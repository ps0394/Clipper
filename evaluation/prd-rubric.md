# PRD Evaluation Rubric

Use this rubric to evaluate PRDs for quality, clarity, and execution readiness. Each PRD section includes a definition and scoring guidance to support consistent reviews.

## Scoring Definitions

- **Strong (3):** Clear, complete, outcome-focused, and ready for execution.
- **Adequate (2):** Present and directionally correct, but needs refinement.
- **Weak (1):** Unclear, incomplete, or misaligned.
- **Missing (0):** Not provided or unusable.

## PRD Sections

### 1. Executive Summary
**Definition:** High-level overview describing what is being built, why it matters, and the intended outcomes.
- **Strong (3):** Clearly explains what is being built, why it matters, and the intended outcomes in plain language.
- **Adequate (2):** Describes the work at a high level but lacks clarity or outcomes.
- **Weak (1):** Vague, overly detailed, or unclear.
- **Missing (0):** Not provided.

### 2. Problem Statement
**Definition:** Description of the user or business problem this work aims to solve.
- **Strong (3):** Clearly defines a real problem supported by data or evidence.
- **Adequate (2):** Problem stated but weakly supported or too broad.
- **Weak (1):** Problem unclear or solution-focused.
- **Missing (0):** Not provided.

### 3. Product Goals & Objectives
**Definition:** Desired outcomes the product or feature is expected to achieve.
- **Strong (3):** Goals are specific, measurable, and outcome-driven.
- **Adequate (2):** Goals present but not measurable or outcome-focused.
- **Weak (1):** Goals vague or activity-based.
- **Missing (0):** Not provided.

### 4. Success Metrics (KRs)
**Definition:** Measures used to determine whether the goals are achieved.
- **Strong (3):** Metrics have clear definitions, baselines, and targets.
- **Adequate (2):** Metrics listed but missing baselines or targets.
- **Weak (1):** Metrics unclear or not measurable.
- **Missing (0):** Not provided.

### 5. Non-Goals / Scope
**Definition:** Explicit items that are out of scope for this work.
- **Strong (3):** Clear non-goals defined to manage expectations.
- **Adequate (2):** Some non-goals listed but incomplete.
- **Weak (1):** Scope boundaries unclear.
- **Missing (0):** Not provided.

### 6. Target Users
**Definition:** Primary and secondary users the product is intended for.
- **Strong (3):** Users clearly defined with relevant context.
- **Adequate (2):** Users identified but lightly described.
- **Weak (1):** Users vague or overly broad.
- **Missing (0):** Not provided.

### 7. Jobs to Be Done / Scenarios
**Definition:** User scenarios describing how users achieve goals using the product.
- **Strong (3):** Clear scenarios focused on user value.
- **Adequate (2):** Scenarios present but unclear or inconsistent.
- **Weak (1):** Scenarios confusing or solution-led.
- **Missing (0):** Not provided.

### 8. Features & Requirements
**Definition:** Description of functionality included in the release.
- **Strong (3):** Features clearly defined with intent and detail.
- **Adequate (2):** Features listed but lack detail.
- **Weak (1):** Features unclear or incomplete.
- **Missing (0):** Not provided.

### 9. Acceptance Criteria
**Definition:** Conditions required for a feature to be considered complete.
- **Strong (3):** Criteria are clear, testable, and scenario-based.
- **Adequate (2):** Criteria present but not fully testable.
- **Weak (1):** Criteria vague or subjective.
- **Missing (0):** Not provided.

### 10. Dependencies & Assumptions
**Definition:** External factors or assumptions that impact delivery.
- **Strong (3):** Dependencies and assumptions clearly identified.
- **Adequate (2):** Some dependencies or assumptions missing.
- **Weak (1):** Dependencies unclear or unrealistic.
- **Missing (0):** Not provided.

### 11. Design & UX
**Definition:** User flows, mockups, and experience design artifacts.
- **Strong (3):** Designs and flows aligned with requirements.
- **Adequate (2):** Designs present but incomplete.
- **Weak (1):** UX unclear or misaligned.
- **Missing (0):** Not provided.

### 12. Technical & Quality Considerations
**Definition:** Technical constraints, performance, security, and compliance needs.
- **Strong (3):** Key considerations clearly addressed.
- **Adequate (2):** High-level only.
- **Weak (1):** Technical risks ignored.
- **Missing (0):** Not provided.

### 13. Release Plan & Milestones
**Definition:** Timeline and sequencing for delivery.
- **Strong (3):** Clear milestones and realistic sequencing.
- **Adequate (2):** High-level timeline only.
- **Weak (1):** Timeline unclear or unrealistic.
- **Missing (0):** Not provided.

### 14. Go-to-Market Considerations
**Definition:** Launch, adoption, and dependency considerations.
- **Strong (3):** GTM activities and dependencies clearly identified.
- **Adequate (2):** GTM mentioned at a high level.
- **Weak (1):** GTM unclear.
- **Missing (0):** Not provided.

### 15. Open Questions & Risks
**Definition:** Outstanding questions or known risks requiring follow-up.
- **Strong (3):** Risks and questions clearly documented.
- **Adequate (2):** Some risks listed.
- **Weak (1):** Risks vague or missing detail.
- **Missing (0):** Not provided.

### 16. Stakeholder Alignment
**Definition:** Confirmation that key stakeholders are aligned.
- **Strong (3):** Stakeholders aligned with evidence of sign-off.
- **Adequate (2):** Stakeholders listed but alignment unclear.
- **Weak (1):** Stakeholder engagement weak.
- **Missing (0):** Not provided.

---

**Maximum Score: 48** (16 sections × 3 points each)
